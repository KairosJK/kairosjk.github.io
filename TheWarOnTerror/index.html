America and the Unwinnable War on Terror

Jonathan Kocevar April 24, 2020 Reading Time: approx. 5 min.

In response to the question: “September 11, 2001 – It has been 18 years since 9/11. Who won the war on terror?”

    Antoine de Saint-Exupéry once said, “War is not an adventure, it is a disease”. No more could be true about the incursions of The United States of America into The Middle East during the war on terror and the resulting occupation. During the 18 years of American involvement in the Middle East, nothing has been completed to fix the area and ultimately is an unwinnable perpetual war in the region. Through the heavy costs of involvement and warfare in the Middle East, loss of support by the people of America and the world, the growing power of ISIL and other terror groups, it is clear that the United States did not win the war on terror.

    The chain of wars in Western Asia for the United States since 9/11 have caused nothing but monetary and human sacrifice for minimal gain. With more that $6 trillion US being spent from 2001-20191, the US has little to show. After taking out the iron-fisted governments of Hussein and Omar the region has seen a power vacuum with many different factions fighting for control. America has been forced to stay and occupy the regions in the name of democracy, paying in lives and money, with no clear end in sight. Annually, without accounting for the replacement of destroyed equipment, America spends over $130 billion US per year since 2001, Analysis before the war put the price of the whole Iraq war, invasion and occupation at $130 US billion compounded. Relating to the human sacrifices of the war on terror, during the war in Iraq and Afghanistan, more than 10,000 American-led coalition forces have been killed,2 and over 300,000 Iraqis and Afghans killed.3 4 Now critics are claiming that the war on terror has reached a stalemate of guerilla warfare and an expensive war of attrition on the United States. Senior researcher Scott Demarais from The Institute of the Study of War reporting that “the Taliban remain on the offensive.”5 The endless cost of the Iraq and Afghan wars on the US economically and in lives with little to no proof of achievements in the area, displays that the war on terror is not being won by America.

    The lengthy and costly occupation of Iraq and Afghanistan has also caused problems domestically and in foreign policy. Politicians and activists around the globe have protested American long drawn imperialism in the Middle East, detest America’s various regionary proxy wars in Syria and Yemen and the tight grasp they have over Iraqi oil fields. Recently, data, such as, The Afghan Papers, have been leaked, the internal documents exposing the US showing a war in Afghanistan was unbeatable and doomed to result into guerilla warfare by the United State’s own generals during the planning phase of the Afghan war.6 The lies and secrets kept from the American people have made much resentment of deployment of United State soldiers in the Middle East. Many anti-occupation groups such as A.N.S.W.E.R (Act Now to Stop War and End Racism) and the Anti-War committee have protested that America end its neo-imperialism in the region, return the Iraqis and Afghans to self governance and end interventionism in the Middle East. The recent heightening of conflict in the region after the drone strike of IRCG top ranking General Qasem Soleimani, killing him on direct orders from American President Donald Trump has brought even more tension to America’s intentions in the East with many countries denouncing the actions committed by the US. Domestically, thousands have began to march in defiance of America’s possession of Afghanistan and Iraq. The biggest demonstration occuring in San Francisco7, marching on the grounds of anti neo-imperialism, neo-colonialism and interventionism, with more than 70% of Americans siding with them, agreeing that America should be out of both Iraq and Afghanistan by the end of this year. Similar protests in Toronto, Berlin, London and Ottawa have also marched in the name of peace for the Middle East. It is clear that the seizure and occupation of the territories involved in the war on terror has lead to nothing but civil unrest and foreign denunciation.

    The “War on Terror” was branded as such with one goal in mind; to destroy and prevent growth of Islamic Terrorism, but has failed in that regard. Initially, Afghanistan was invaded to destroy the root and main base of the Al-Qaeda insurgency, the terrorists behind 9/11 backed by Osama Bin Laden. Iraq was invaded due to the alleged dealings with Al-Qaeda by the former President of Iraq, Saddam Hussein8. The war in Afghanistan had been a tactical and strategic nightmare for the United States general command. Since the invasion in October of 2001, the insurgency and splinter cell groups of the dissolved Al-Qaeda raid Afghanistan freely. While the initial invasion of Iraq was executed very efficiently and quickly, Saddam’s dictatorship fell to the Nato Coalition within a month but the US failed to replace the Ba’athist Iraq with a new western style government and soon Iraq fell into the same problem of guerilla warfare and insurgent factions as Afghanistan had previously.. Before the invasion of Iraq, Saddam Hussein had a solid grip on society, media, and daily life of Iraqi citizens. While harsh, Saddam reformed Iraq into a secular and Arab powerhouse of a country, holding a tight fist over Islamic insurgents and terrorists. With the invasion and the mismanagement of the US officials to replace Ba’ath party members and set up a working government after the initial invasion, many soldiers of Hussein’s old Iraqi guard had become insurgents controlling Northern Iraq and Kurdistan, creating Al-Qaeda splinter cell group ISIS.9 While America’s supposed goal in Iraq was to stop the growth of terrorism in Iraq, it turned it into a safeheaven instead for Al-Qaeda and the remenants of the Taliban. Iironically, the once low crime country turned into a battleground for Muslim factions, with terror attacks rising exponentialy within only weeks of the initial invasion. With America’s incursion into Iraq and following occupation also came the greatest recruiting tool for Islamic terrorist groups. Many terror groups saw thousands of jihadists from Pakistan to Bosnia come to fight against America and install an Islamic caliphate in Iraq10, only adding more fuel to the fire to the occupation of Iraq. The failure to address these problems during the initial invasion phase has left both Iraq and Afghanistan as some of the most dangerous places on earth, having forced the US to keep occupying these countries after failing to replace their governments and military.

    In closing, the war on terror has been nothing but attrition with little to no profit for the United States, and has ultimately lost to the guerilla factions within Iraq and Afghanistan. This is clear due to the immense costs of involvement and warfare in the Middle East, loss of support domestically and in foriegn relations retaining to the area, the growing power of ISIL and other terror groups, it is clear that the United States has lost the war on terror.
